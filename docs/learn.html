Part 1

The 12-area map

Counts are descriptive within the current taxonomy. Open each area for its methods, evaluation questions, and paper starting point.

1LLM Reasoning, Post-Training, and RLVR
1,099 papers · 16.6%

Studies reward-driven adaptation, reasoning traces, verifiers, test-time search, preference learning, and alternative decoding.

GRPOprocess rewardstest-time computepass@k

Representative paper: The Flexibility Trap tests whether arbitrary token order helps diffusion-language-model reasoning.

Watch for: broad LLM training papers being mistaken for reasoning papers.

2Multimodal, Vision, and Perception
889 papers · 13.4%

Moves from recognition toward grounded visual reasoning, video, spatial understanding, 3D structure, and robust perception.

VLMsvideo3Dgrounding

Representative paper: Motion Attribution for Video Generation isolates which training clips influence motion rather than appearance.

Watch for: answers produced from language priors without real visual evidence.

3Theory, Optimization, and Algorithms
737 papers · 11.1%

Develops mathematical explanations, learning guarantees, regret bounds, optimization methods, and theories of modern architectures.

generalizationregretoptimizationexpressivity

Representative paper: To Grok Grokking gives quantitative bounds for delayed generalization in ridge regression.

Watch for: correct theorems whose assumptions do not match practical model scales.

4AI for Science, Health, and Neuro
587 papers · 8.9%

Applies ML to proteins, molecules, physical systems, climate, health data, time series, and neural signals.

proteinsscientific MLtime seriesuncertainty

Representative paper: Protein Autoregressive Modeling generates backbones from coarse topology to atomic detail.

Watch for: benchmark validity being confused with scientific or clinical usefulness.

5Data-Centric, Causal, and Federated ML
526 papers · 7.9%

Improves supervision, continual learning, causal reasoning, and distributed learning across separate clients.

data qualitycausalitycontinual learningfederation

Representative paper: Pretrained VLA Models Are Surprisingly Resistant to Forgetting studies replay and pretraining in sequential robot learning.

Watch for: predictive association being described as causal evidence.

6Systems and Efficient Foundation Models
515 papers · 7.8%

Targets training stability, serving, quantization, compression, long context, caching, hardware, and end-to-end cost.

training systemsquantizationKV cachethroughput

Representative paper: Controlled LLM Training on Spectral Sphere constrains weights and updates for stable large-scale optimization.

Watch for: isolated speedups that disappear in the full application.

7Safety, Governance, Privacy, and Society
502 papers · 7.6%

Studies attacks, alignment, oversight, privacy, fairness, governance, and the social effects of deployed models.

red teamingmonitoringprivacyoversight

Representative paper: The Obfuscation Atlas studies whether training against deception detectors creates honesty or evasion.

Watch for: narrow safety tests being treated as complete real-world threat models.

8Agents, Code, and Tool Use
496 papers · 7.5%

Builds systems that plan, use tools, write or inspect code, remember context, and interact with structured environments.

planningtoolscode agentsmemory

Representative paper: PaperBanana orchestrates agents for academic illustration and introduces a 292-case benchmark.

Watch for: better scaffolding being mistaken for better learned capability.

9Graphs, Geometry, and Representation Learning
391 papers · 5.9%

Uses relations, symmetry, shape, manifolds, and structured representations in models and theory.

GNNsequivariancegeometrymessage passing

Representative paper: Which Algorithms Can Graph Neural Networks Learn? characterizes generalization and impossibility for algorithm execution.

Watch for: fitting small examples being confused with learning a general algorithm.

10Generative Modeling
379 papers · 5.7%

Studies diffusion, flow, sampling, control, quality, consistency, and efficiency across generated media and scientific data.

diffusionflow matchingsamplingguidance

Representative paper: A Random Matrix Perspective on the Consistency of Diffusion Models explains cross-split sample similarity.

Watch for: practical media quality and mathematical sampling theory being collapsed into one objective.

11Reinforcement Learning and Control
312 papers · 4.7%

Covers online and offline RL, control, exploration, robustness, planning, policy learning, and decision-time computation.

offline RLactor-criticcontrolplanning

Representative paper: On Computation and Reinforcement Learning separates parameter count from policy computation.

Watch for: LLM post-training being mistaken for the whole RL field.

12Robotics, Embodiment, and World Models
195 papers · 2.9%

Connects multimodal perception, memory, action, manipulation, world prediction, and physical constraints.

VLAmemorymanipulationworld models

Representative paper: RoboMME compares 14 memory-augmented policy variants across 16 history-dependent tasks.

Watch for: compelling demos hiding narrow tasks, resets, or weak generalization.

Part 2

Six patterns across areas

A conference story comes from relationships between signals, not only a ranking of paper counts.

Trend 1

Reasoning after pretraining

The largest mapped area combines rewards, verifiers, search, and test-time compute. Boundary review remains important.

Trend 2

Systems and agents

Models increasingly depend on efficient infrastructure, memory, tools, and execution environments.

Trend 3

Program vs public

Theory and safety have stronger oral enrichment than AlphaXiv attention, showing that signals highlight different work.

Trend 4

Robotics visibility

Robotics is small by mapped share and unusually high in AlphaXiv attention. This is not a quality ranking.

Trend 5

Venue context

Multimodal and vision is large inside ICML but underweight against this project's nearby-venue baseline.

Trend 6

Artifact visibility

A visible repository link can guide inspection, but reproducibility requires identity, contents, execution, and result checks.
