Method and trust

How this project builds the map

A transparent view of sources, evidence strength, limitations, and ways to contribute.

1. Official conference corpus

The analysis begins with 6,628 ICML 2026 paper records and official program labels.

2. Navigation taxonomy

Semantic clusters are mapped into 12 broad areas. The map is useful for exploration but contains review boundaries.

3. Several evidence streams

Paper share, program selection, AlphaXiv attention, historical venue comparisons, and visible artifact links answer different questions.

4. Conservative teaching

Lessons state a caveat beside major patterns and avoid turning directional evidence into settled causal claims.

Learning design

What makes this a course

The public experience is designed around comprehension and transfer, not page views.

Explain before naming

Every area starts with a problem and mental model before introducing specialist terms.

Make it concrete

Technical pipelines, compact formulas, and worked examples connect intuition to methods.

Require retrieval

Progress is earned by answering at least three of four lesson questions correctly.

Read real papers

Each area includes one deep case and two conservative abstract-based comparisons.

Teach limitations

Every major result is paired with what the evidence does not establish.

Recommend review

The final assessment links missed concepts back to the relevant course modules.

Trust levels

Not all evidence is equal

Checked facts

Official rows, oral and award flags, and direct generated counts.

Directional patterns

Cross-source area signals that help choose what to read next.

Open questions

Taxonomy boundaries, causal explanations, and reproducibility claims requiring human review.

Important current limitation

The manual claim and area review queues are not complete. Public materials are designed for learning and research triage, not as a publication-ready ranking of papers or fields.
