Reinforcement Learning and Control

Reinforcement learning studies sequential decisions whose consequences may arrive later, under uncertainty and limited experience.

Start with intuition

The mental model

An agent observes a state, chooses an action using a policy, receives reward, and transitions to a new state. Learning must assign credit across time.

Inside this area

Core and offline RL; online learning and exploration; policy optimization; robust RL; control; model-based learning and planning.

Common methods

Value and policy learning; actor-critic; offline objectives; model-free planning; regret analysis; robust optimization; compute-adaptive policies.

Technical core

A common research pipeline

  1. 1Define states, actions, transitions, and rewards
  2. 2Collect online experience or use an offline dataset
  3. 3Estimate values or improve a policy
  4. 4Evaluate return, robustness, sample cost, and generalization

Make it concrete

Worked example: more computation per action

  1. Keep the policy parameter count fixed.
  2. Allow the policy to perform more internal update steps before acting.
  3. Test whether it solves longer-horizon tasks.
  4. Report wall-clock cost as well as reward, because internal computation is not free.

Do not overclaim

Evaluation

How progress is tested

Return and sample efficiency; generalization across horizons; offline-to-online transfer; robustness; safety; compute and parameter budgets.

Useful comparison

Supervised learning receives target labels. RL receives consequences of actions. Offline RL must learn without freely exploring beyond a fixed behavior dataset.

Paper lab

Read three cases with a purpose

The core paper receives a complete problem-to-limit walkthrough. The next two cases are curated to show important breadth within the area.

Paper 1 · Core caseOral

On Computation and Reinforcement Learning

Problem
Standard RL architectures mix parameter count with computation, making it hard to ask whether a fixed-size policy benefits from more compute.
Method
Formalize compute-bounded policies, prove capability and horizon-generalization separations, and build a variable-compute minimal architecture.
Evidence
Theory plus 31 online and offline RL tasks, comparing extra compute with feed-forward and residual policies using up to five times more parameters.
Main result
A fixed-parameter policy can solve harder and longer-horizon tasks by spending more computation at decision time.
Important limit
The chosen tasks and architecture may not capture wall-clock latency, environment interaction cost, or complex continuous-control deployments.

Paper 2 · Abstract-based previewComparison case

Learning Human-Robot Collaboration via Heterogeneous-Agent Lyapunov Policy Optimization

Why selected: Stable human-robot policy learning broadens the core case and shows another important part of this area.

Problem
Independent learning by humans and robots can oscillate because the two agents follow different objectives and response dynamics.
Approach
Project decentralized policy gradients so a Lyapunov measure of parameter disagreement contracts at every step.
Evidence
Stability analysis, simulations, and physical humanoid-robot collaboration experiments.
Takeaway
Certified contraction is reported to improve robustness and generalization in difficult collaboration cases.
Caution
Parameter-space stability is not the same as human safety, comfort, or successful transfer to broader interaction settings.
Metadata cues
RL / policy optimization; Agents / tool use; Bayesian / probabilistic · robotics/embodied

Paper 3 · Abstract-based previewComparison case

Distributional Inverse Reinforcement Learning

Why selected: Risk-aware inverse RL broadens the core case and shows another important part of this area.

Problem
Conventional inverse RL often recovers one reward or matches only mean return, hiding uncertainty and risk preferences.
Approach
Model distributions over rewards and returns using stochastic-dominance constraints and risk-sensitive policy learning.
Evidence
Convergence analysis plus synthetic, neurobehavioral, and MuJoCo imitation experiments.
Takeaway
Distributional reward representations are reported to improve risk-aware imitation across varied settings.
Caution
Recovered reward distributions remain model-dependent explanations of behavior, not direct measurements of human intent.
Metadata cues
RL / policy optimization · robotics/embodied; theory/synthetic
