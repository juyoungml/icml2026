Generative Modeling

Generative models learn how data is distributed so they can produce, transform, or score new samples—not merely assign a label.

Start with intuition

The mental model

Diffusion gradually corrupts data and learns to reverse that process. Flow methods learn a continuous transport from a simple distribution to the data distribution.

Inside this area

Diffusion sampling and inverse problems; flow models; image and video generation; controllable generation; sampling theory and consistency.

Common methods

Score matching; denoising diffusion; flow matching; transport; guidance; distillation; random matrix analysis; efficient sampling.

Technical core

A common research pipeline

  1. 1Choose a simple starting distribution
  2. 2Learn a denoising score or transport field
  3. 3Run a multi-step or distilled sampler
  4. 4Evaluate quality, diversity, control, speed, and consistency separately
Denoising stepxₜ₋₁ = xₜ + model-predicted correction + controlled noise

The sampler repeatedly moves a noisy state toward regions that resemble training data.

Make it concrete

Worked example: same seed, different training split

  1. Train two diffusion models on different samples from one data distribution.
  2. Give both models the same initial noise.
  3. Their outputs can remain surprisingly similar.
  4. Random-matrix analysis asks how shared data statistics and finite-sample differences explain agreement.
Pause and predict

Do speedups preserve quality under realistic inference budgets?

Look for evidence tied to sample quality and diversity; likelihood or bounds; speed; consistency across seeds and data splits; controllability; human preference; physical plausibility. Evaluation

How progress is tested

Sample quality and diversity; likelihood or bounds; speed; consistency across seeds and data splits; controllability; human preference; physical plausibility.

Useful comparison

Likelihood, perceptual quality, diversity, prompt adherence, physical validity, and sampling speed answer different questions. No single metric captures them all.

Paper 1 · Core caseOutstanding Paper Honorable Mention · oral

A Random Matrix Perspective on the Consistency of Diffusion Models

Problem
Diffusion models trained on different data splits can produce surprisingly similar samples from the same noise seed. Why?
Method
Build a random-matrix framework for finite-data effects on linear denoisers and sampling maps, including expectations, variance, and sampling trajectories.
Evidence
Closed-form predictions for linear models and empirical validation on UNet and DiT architectures in a non-memorization regime.
Main result
Shared Gaussian statistics explain much cross-split consistency; disagreement depends on spectral anisotropy, input inhomogeneity, and dataset size.
Important limit
The sharp theory is linear and Gaussian; nonlinear modern generators may depart from it through feature learning, memorization, or conditioning.

Paper 2 · Abstract-based previewComparison case

High-accuracy sampling for diffusion models and log-concave distributions

Problem
Existing high-accuracy diffusion samplers can require a number of steps that grows polynomially as target error shrinks.
Approach
Develop algorithms whose accuracy dependence is polylogarithmic under stated score-estimation and distribution assumptions.
Evidence
Complexity theorems for general, Lipschitz, intrinsic-dimensional, and log-concave settings.
Takeaway
The analysis provides an exponential improvement in error dependence over earlier theoretical guarantees.
Caution
Iteration complexity assumes sufficiently accurate score access and does not directly measure wall-clock quality for modern image models.

Paper 3 · Abstract-based previewComparison case

High-accuracy and dimension-free sampling with diffusions

Problem
Numerically solving diffusion dynamics at high accuracy often needs many small discretization steps tied to ambient dimension.
Approach
Combine low-degree approximation with collocation to solve the diffusion equation more efficiently.
Evidence
A theoretical iteration bound with polylogarithmic accuracy dependence and dimension entering only through effective support radius.
Takeaway
The solver establishes a route to high-accuracy guarantees without explicit ambient-dimension dependence.
Caution
The guarantee concerns approximate score access and mathematical error, which may differ from perceptual quality and GPU runtime.
