## Learning Outcome Coverage

The course tests whether a learner can:

- describe the 12 broad research areas;
- explain six major conference patterns;
- distinguish five kinds of evidence;
- interpret new examples rather than only repeat memorized facts;
- read a paper through its problem, contribution, evidence, limit, and wider relevance;
- state disagreements and uncertainty fairly;
- prepare a short evidence-based conference briefing.

Every outcome appears in at least one lesson checkpoint and one final quiz section.

## Assessment Audit

The final quiz contains:

- 15 points for key ideas and terms;
- 20 points for the conference map;
- 25 points for evidence interpretation;
- 20 points for unfamiliar paper cases;
- 20 points for synthesis and communication.

The passing rule requires 80 out of 100, at least half the available points in every part, and correction of serious interpretation errors. This prevents a learner from passing through memorization while misunderstanding public attention, program selection, or artifact visibility.

## Evidence Safety Audit

The course repeatedly distinguishes:

- official counts from taxonomy interpretation;
- oral and award selection from complete quality judgment;
- AlphaXiv attention from scientific importance;
- historical comparison from causal explanation;
- visible repository links from reproducibility;
- locally inspected PDF notes from completed human review.

The roadmap clearly states that 310 manual claim and area review rows remain unfilled. Trend lessons therefore use cautious confidence labels.

## Clarity Audit

A rough sentence check over the main learner-facing files found:

- average sentence lengths between about 10 and 16 words;
- no long sentences in the glossary;
- short lesson sections with repeated labels;
- technical terms explained in the glossary;
- examples placed before or beside interpretation;
- a practical activity after every main learning unit.

The paper course necessarily contains technical paper titles and method names. Explanations around those terms use shorter, direct sentences.

## Navigation Audit

The course contains no missing local links. The generated dashboard includes a Newcomer Roadmap link. The generated project index places the learning path before the researcher-facing material.

## Structured Learner Walk-Through

An internal cold-start walk-through followed the standard route without using the project index:

1. The roadmap linked directly to every required lesson.
2. Each lesson stated a goal or reason for reading.
3. Each learning stage ended with a check or activity.
4. The quiz linked directly to the answer and review guide.
5. The answer guide linked learners back to the relevant lessons.
6. A learner could complete the route without opening the larger research workspace.

A human pilot reader is still recommended for the next revision. Their feedback should focus on unclear terms, time estimates, paper difficulty, and whether the final quiz feels fair.
